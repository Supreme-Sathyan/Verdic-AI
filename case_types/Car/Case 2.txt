This complaint is filed under Section 12 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, praying this Commission to direct the Opposite Party (i) to repair the mechanical defects of the demo car; (ii) to compensate the complainant for the faulty demo vehicle; (iii) to bear the costs of the case.

The complainant, Ms. Priya Kapoor, test-drove a demo car at XYZ Motors and noticed that the brakes and transmission were faulty. Despite the dealership assuring her that the vehicle was in perfect condition, the mechanical issues persisted after she took the car for a second test drive. The complainant argues that the dealership should have ensured the demo car was in proper working order.

Point No.2:

In the result, the complaint is partly allowed, and the Opposite Party (XYZ Motors) is directed –

(i) To repair all mechanical issues of the demo car.

(ii) To compensate the complainant with ₹2,00,000 for the faulty demo vehicle.

(iii) To bear the costs of litigation incurred by the complainant.
