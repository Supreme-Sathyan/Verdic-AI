This complaint is filed under Section 12 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, praying this Commission to direct the Opposite Party (i) to repair the damage caused to the vehicle during transportation; (ii) to compensate the complainant for the distress caused; (iii) to bear the costs of the case.

The complainant, Ms. Shilpa Mehra, purchased a car from a dealership and had it delivered to her home. Upon receiving the car, Ms. Mehra discovered a large dent on the side door and damage to the rear bumper. The delivery service company failed to notify the complainant of any damages that occurred during transportation.

Point No.2:

In the result, the complaint is partly allowed, and the Opposite Party (ABC Delivery Service) is directed –

(i) To repair the vehicle at their own cost.

(ii) To compensate the complainant with ₹30,000 for the inconvenience caused.

(iii) To bear the costs of litigation incurred by the complainant.
