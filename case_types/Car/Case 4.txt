This complaint is filed under Section 12 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, praying this Commission to direct the Opposite Party (i) to repair the damaged demo car; (ii) to compensate the complainant for the inconvenience; (iii) to bear the costs of the case.

The complainant, Ms. Anjali Mehra, purchased a new demo car from XYZ Car Dealership. However, upon delivery, the car was found to have several scratches and dents that were not present during the demonstration. The complainant claims the dealership is responsible for the damage during transport.

Point No.2:

In the result, the complaint is partly allowed, and the Opposite Party (XYZ Car Dealership) is directed –

(i) To repair the car at no cost to the complainant.

(ii) To compensate the complainant with ₹1,50,000 for the inconvenience caused.

(iii) To bear the costs of litigation incurred by the complainant.
