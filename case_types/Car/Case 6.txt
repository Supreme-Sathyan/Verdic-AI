
This complaint is filed under Section 12 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, praying this Commission to direct the Opposite Party (i) to compensate the complainant for the damage caused by vandalism; (ii) to bear the costs of the case.

The complainant, Mr. Vijay Singh, parked his car in a public parking lot owned by the local municipality. When he returned, he found that the car had been vandalized—windows were broken, and graffiti was sprayed on the bodywork. Despite the parking lot being in a well-lit area, the municipality claimed there were no security measures in place to prevent such incidents.

Point No.2:

In the result, the complaint is partly allowed, and the Opposite Party (Municipal Corporation) is directed –

(i) To compensate the complainant with ₹50,000 for the damage to the car.

(ii) To improve security measures in the parking lot.

(iii) To bear the costs of litigation incurred by the complainant.
