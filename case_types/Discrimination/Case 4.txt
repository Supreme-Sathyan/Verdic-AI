This complaint is filed under Section 12 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, praying this Commission to direct the Opposite Party (i) to provide appropriate healthcare services without discrimination; (ii) to compensate the complainant for emotional distress; (iii) to bear the costs of the case.

The complainant, Ms. Tanya Sood, a transgender woman, was denied medical treatment at a private hospital, with the staff refusing to acknowledge her gender identity and offering her no medical services due to her transition status.

Point No.2:

In the result, the complaint is partly allowed, and the Opposite Party (Private Hospital) is directed –

(i) To provide the complainant with appropriate healthcare services.

(ii) To compensate the complainant with ₹5,00,000 for emotional distress.

(iii) To bear the costs of litigation incurred by the complainant.
